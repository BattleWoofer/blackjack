The goal of Blackjack is to get your score as close to, or equal to, twenty-one, without going over.
Aces can be worth 11 or 1, face cards are 10, all others equal card number.

Step One: Player draws two cards. An ace and a 10-card make for a natural Blackjack, a 3x pot payout, but if the house matches draw, you lose. If card total is 9 through 11, you can double your bet.
Step Two: House draws one card face-up, one face-down. If the face-up risks Blackjack, the face-down is revealed. Otherwise, move to player turn.
Step Three: Player "hits" to draw cards and add points, but going over 21 will cause loss. "Stand" when desired points are reached.
Step Four: House draws until it reaches 17 points, or goes over 21. For the former, highest point value wins.
